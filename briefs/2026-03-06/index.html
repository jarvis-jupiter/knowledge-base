🌍 World

US-Israel-Iran War Escalates, Oil Markets Rattled

Israeli airstrikes have struck Tehran and Beirut, while the US attacked an Iranian drone carrier at sea. Iran has retaliated with missiles and drones across the region, putting the Strait of Hormuz — through which one-fifth of global oil flows — under severe strain. Brent crude sits stubbornly at ~$84/barrel, with Morgan Stanley warning of significant market volatility ahead.

Source: The Hindu, Modern Diplomacy

China Sets Its Lowest Growth Target Since 1991

Beijing has pegged GDP growth at 4.5–5% for 2026 — the least ambitious target in over three decades. Weak household spending, a stagnant property sector, and geopolitical headwinds from both the Middle East conflict and continued US trade pressure are cited as driving factors. The lower target gives Beijing room to manoeuvre without appearing to stumble.

Source: Reuters

ECB's Lagarde Warns of 1920s-Style Economic Fragmentation

Christine Lagarde cautioned that the collapse of the rules-based world order, compounded by AI disruption, echoes the dangerous economic conditions of the interwar period. She called on global leaders to establish a basic framework for cooperation, warning that geopolitical fragmentation in an AI-driven economy carries ruinous costs.

Source: The Edge Singapore

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

Reeves' Spring Statement: Growth Slashed, Headroom Gained

The OBR revised UK growth down to 1.1% for 2026 (from 1.4%), though public borrowing has fallen by nearly £18bn compared to the Autumn forecast — the lowest in six years. Reeves has £24bn of fiscal headroom and is pressing ahead with the two-child benefit cap repeal from April.

Source: City A.M., Gov.uk

Middle East Conflict Casts Shadow Over Economic Outlook

Reeves acknowledged in her Spring Statement that escalating conflict in the Middle East poses the primary risk to her economic plan, particularly through energy price shocks. Inflation is still expected to hit the 2% target by late 2026, though the path is increasingly fragile given Brent crude at $84.

Source: The Guardian, HuffPost UK

🇧🇷 Brazil

Election Season Officially Begins — Party Window Opens

Brazil's 30-day janela partidária opened on 5th March, allowing federal and state deputies to switch parties without losing their seats. With Lula confirmed for a fourth-term bid and Bolsonaro ineligible (27-year sentence for attempted coup), the race is fragmenting fast. At least 20 ministers are reportedly considering candidacies to shore up the governing coalition.

Source: TRE-SP, Al Jazeera

Petrobras Profits Soar; Trade Surplus Holds Strong

Petrobras announced profits nearly tripled in 2025 on record oil and gas output, with offshore expansion plans on the Equatorial Margin advancing despite environmental opposition. Brazil's February trade surplus came in at US$4.2bn — its fourth-best February on record — though the real remains under pressure with the dollar at R$5.28.

Source: Agência Brasil, Folha de S.Paulo
